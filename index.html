Microservices with Reliable Events

Applied event-driven patterns with SAGA/outbox to avoid distributed transactions and improve failure isolation. Emphasized idempotency, retries, DLQs, and traceability.

Impact: Better fault tolerance, clearer service ownership, predictable recovery.

Platform Observability & Operations

Impact: Faster incident triage and fewer “unknown” outages.

Infrastructure as Code

Impact: Reliable environment parity and easier scaling/changes.

Modernized a Legacy .NET Service

Impact: Improved maintainability and reduced production issues.

Performance & Reliability Improvements

How I Work

Signals seniority quickly

  • I design for failure: timeouts, backpressure, retries (carefully), and graceful degradation.
  • I prioritize operability: logs, metrics, traces, and dashboards are part of the architecture.
  • I use managed services intentionally to reduce operational burden and improve reliability.
  • I document trade-offs and align architecture with business constraints and delivery timelines.
  • I write production-ready code: correctness first, then performance and elegance.
  • I keep boundaries clean: small services, clear contracts, and readable abstractions.
  • I build with observability: structured logs, correlation IDs, and health checks by default.
  • I prefer iterative modernization over risky rewrites, and I mentor teams through it.
